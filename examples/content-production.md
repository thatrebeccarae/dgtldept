# Workflow: Research-to-Distribution Content Pipeline

**Skills used:** Research Digest, ICP Research, SEO Content Writer, Content Workflow
**Time:** ~1 hour hands-on
**Output:** SEO-optimized article with social distribution pack

---

## The Scenario

You need to publish a high-quality blog post on a trending topic in your space — research-backed, SEO-optimized, written in your brand voice, and packaged for social distribution. Instead of spending a full day, you chain four skills to go from topic to published-ready in about an hour.

## Step 1: Research the Topic (Research Digest)

Start with a structured research brief so the content has substance, not just opinions.

```
Research [topic] — pull from industry sources, recent publications,
and any relevant data. I need:
- Key data points and statistics (with sources)
- Counter-arguments and nuanced takes
- Content angles that aren't already saturated
- Credibility assessment of major sources
```

**What you get:** A structured research brief with sourced data points, credibility ratings, and 3-4 content angles ranked by originality.

## Step 2: Ground It in the Audience (ICP Research)

Make sure the content speaks to the right people.

```
I'm writing about [topic] for [target audience]. Build a quick ICP
focused on content consumption:
- What problems does this audience care about related to [topic]?
- What language do they use to describe these problems?
- Where do they consume content? (LinkedIn, newsletters, podcasts, etc.)
- What content formats perform best for this audience?
```

**What you get:** Audience-specific language, pain points, and format preferences that shape the article's angle and tone — not a generic persona, but a content-consumption profile.

## Step 3: Write the Article (SEO Content Writer)

Now write with research and audience data loaded.

```
Write a [word count] article on [topic].

Research brief: [paste or reference Step 1 output]
Target audience: [paste or reference Step 2 output]
Target keyword: [primary keyword]
Secondary keywords: [2-3 supporting keywords]

Brand voice: [describe or reference brand guide]
Goal: [what the reader should do/feel/understand after reading]
```

**What you get:** An SEO-optimized article with proper heading structure, keyword placement, internal linking suggestions, and a meta description — written in the audience's language with research-backed claims.

## Step 4: Package for Distribution (Content Workflow)

Turn the article into a distribution-ready package.

```
Take this article and create a distribution pack:
- LinkedIn post (hook + value + CTA format)
- Twitter/X thread (5-7 tweets)
- Email subject lines (3 options, A/B test ready)
- Pull quotes for social graphics
- Internal linking recommendations
```

**What you get:** Platform-specific content adapted for each channel's format and audience expectations — not just the article copy-pasted with different character limits.

---

## Why This Works

Each step constrains the next in a productive way:

```
Research Digest → grounded in data, not opinions
    ↓
ICP Research → shaped by audience language and pain points
    ↓
SEO Content Writer → optimized for search with audience-native voice
    ↓
Content Workflow → adapted per platform, not copy-pasted
```

Starting with research prevents the "generic AI blog post" problem. Adding ICP data prevents the "technically correct but doesn't resonate" problem. Running SEO optimization with both inputs prevents the "ranks but doesn't convert" problem. And distribution packaging prevents the "published but nobody saw it" problem.

## Variations

- **Add Brand DNA:** Before Step 3, run Brand DNA on the client's website to extract voice, colors, and values — then feed the brand profile into the SEO Content Writer
- **Skip ICP:** If you already have a documented ICP, jump straight from Research to Writing
- **Add Email Composer:** After the distribution pack, draft an outreach email to industry contacts who might share or link to the article
- **Series mode:** Run Steps 1-2 once, then repeat Steps 3-4 for multiple articles from the same research brief — each targeting a different angle from the brief
