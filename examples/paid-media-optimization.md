# Workflow: Paid Media Waste-to-Action Pipeline

**Skills used:** Wasted Spend Finder, Competitor Ads Analyst, Google Ads, Account Structure Review
**Time:** ~30 minutes hands-on
**Output:** Uploadable exclusion lists, competitive intelligence, and a restructuring roadmap

---

## The Scenario

Ad spend is climbing but ROAS is flat. You suspect waste but don't know where. This workflow finds the waste, shows you what competitors are doing differently, audits the full account, and gives you a structural fix — not just band-aids.

## Step 1: Find the Waste (Wasted Spend Finder)

Start with the hardest question: where is money being burned?

```
Analyze wasted spend in this Google Ads account for the last 90 days.
Here's the search terms report: [paste or attach CSV]

Categorize waste thematically — I want to understand *why* we're
wasting, not just *where*.
```

**What you get:** Thematic waste categories (brand confusion, irrelevant intent, geographic mismatch, etc.) with spend totals per category and ready-to-upload negative keyword lists.

## Step 2: See What's Working for Competitors (Competitor Ads Analyst)

With waste patterns identified, check what competitors are doing that you're not.

```
Pull competitor ads for [competitor 1], [competitor 2], and [competitor 3]
from the Meta Ad Library and Google Ads Transparency Center.

Cross-reference with the waste analysis — are we spending on
terms/audiences our competitors have already abandoned? Are they
targeting angles we're missing?
```

**What you get:** Competitor creative analysis, messaging pattern extraction, positioning gaps, and specific angles your competitors use that you don't — filtered through the lens of your waste data.

## Step 3: Full Account Audit (Google Ads)

Now audit the entire account structure with context from Steps 1-2.

```
Run a scored Google Ads audit on this account. I've already identified
waste patterns and competitive gaps:

[reference or paste waste and competitor findings]

Score every dimension (A-F) and highlight where the waste patterns
trace back to structural problems — not just keyword issues.
```

**What you get:** A 74-check scored audit (A-F per dimension) that connects waste patterns to root causes — campaign structure, bidding strategy, audience targeting, and conversion setup.

## Step 4: Build the Restructuring Plan (Account Structure Review)

Turn findings into an actionable restructuring roadmap.

```
Based on the audit, waste analysis, and competitive gaps, build a
restructuring plan for this account. Include:
- Which campaigns to consolidate (with conversion volume thresholds)
- Budget reallocation from waste categories to high-opportunity areas
- Structural changes needed before scaling spend
- 30/60/90 day implementation timeline
```

**What you get:** A prioritized restructuring roadmap with consolidation recommendations, budget reallocation math, and a phased timeline.

---

## Why This Works

The pipeline moves from *diagnosis* to *intelligence* to *audit* to *action*:

```
Wasted Spend Finder → where money is being burned
    ↓
Competitor Ads Analyst → what others do differently (informed by waste patterns)
    ↓
Google Ads Audit → root cause analysis (informed by waste + competitive data)
    ↓
Account Structure Review → restructuring plan (informed by all three)
```

Running the audit *first* is a common mistake — you'd get a generic score without understanding the specific waste patterns driving it. By finding waste first, the audit becomes diagnostic rather than descriptive.

## Variations

- **Add Meta Ads:** Run a parallel Meta Ads audit at Step 3 and feed both into the restructuring plan
- **Add Cross-Platform Audit:** Replace Step 3 with a Cross-Platform Audit for a unified health score across Google, Meta, and Microsoft
- **Add Pro Deck Builder:** Package the full analysis into a client-ready deck at the end
- **Monthly cadence:** Run Step 1 monthly as a hygiene check — the exclusion lists are directly uploadable
