# Email Composer — Examples

## Example 1: Cold Outreach to Potential Client

**Prompt:**
> Write a cold outreach email to a DTC brand founder. We're a performance marketing agency and noticed their Meta ads have been running the same creative for 3 months (visible in the Ad Library).

**Expected output:**
```
Subject: Noticed something in your Meta Ad Library

Hi [Name],

I was looking at [Brand]'s Meta ads and noticed your main creatives have been live since December. That's not a criticism — it means they're working. But after 3 months, creative fatigue is likely costing you 15-25% in efficiency.

We ran a similar refresh for a DTC skincare brand last month and cut their CPA by 31% with 4 new variants.

Would it be useful if I put together a quick creative audit? No pitch — just what I'd test and why.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Example 2: Deliverable Handoff

**Prompt:**
> Write an email handing off a Klaviyo audit report to a client. Key findings: post-purchase flow is underperforming (RPR $0.07 vs. $1.00 benchmark), list is 65% unengaged, and welcome series has no SMS.

**Expected output:**
```
Subject: Klaviyo Audit — 3 Revenue Opportunities Identified

Hi [Name],

Attached is your Klaviyo audit covering flows, list health, and automation performance.

Key findings:
- Post-purchase flow RPR is $0.07 vs. $1.00 benchmark — the biggest revenue opportunity
- 65% of your list hasn't engaged in 90+ days — suppressing them will improve deliverability immediately
- Welcome series has no SMS — adding it typically lifts welcome flow revenue 20-30%

I'd recommend we start with the post-purchase flow rebuild as the highest-impact, lowest-effort win.

Want to walk through it on Thursday? I can share a screen and go slide by slide.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Example 3: Meeting Follow-Up with Action Items

**Prompt:**
> Write a follow-up email after a kickoff meeting. We discussed timeline (8 weeks), first deliverable (GA4 audit in week 2), and the client needs to grant us Google Analytics access.

**Expected output:**
```
Subject: Kickoff follow-up — action items and next steps

Hi [Name],

Thanks for the kickoff today. Here's what we aligned on:

**Timeline:** 8 weeks, starting [date]

**Action items:**
- [ ] [Client name] — Grant GA4 access to [email] — by [date]
- [ ] [Your name] — Deliver GA4 audit — by end of week 2
- [ ] [Your name] — Send tracking spec template — by Friday

**Next check-in:** [Date] at [Time]

The sooner we get GA4 access, the sooner we can start the audit. Let me know if you hit any snags on the permissions side.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Example 4: Scope Change Discussion

**Prompt:**
> Write an email to a client about scope creep. They asked us to also manage their TikTok ads, which wasn't in the SOW. We're happy to do it but it adds $2K/month.

**Expected output:**
```
Subject: TikTok Ads — Scope addition for your review

Hi [Name],

Great idea to expand into TikTok — the creative we're producing for Meta will translate well.

TikTok management wasn't part of our current SOW, so I want to make sure we're aligned before we start:

**What's included:**
- Campaign setup, creative adaptation, and ongoing optimization
- Weekly reporting alongside your Google/Meta dashboards

**Additional cost:** $2,000/month

**Alternative:** If budget is tight, we could pause Display prospecting (lowest ROAS channel) and reallocate that management time to TikTok at no additional cost.

Which approach works better for you? Happy to discuss on our next call.

Best,
[Your Name]
```
