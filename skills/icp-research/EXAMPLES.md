# ICP Research — Examples

## Example 1: B2B SaaS ICP

**Prompt:**
> Build an ICP for our marketing analytics platform. We sell to mid-market DTC e-commerce brands ($5M-$50M revenue). Our main competitors are Triple Whale, Northbeam, and Rockerbox.

**Expected output:** Complete ICP profile including:
- **Persona:** "Sarah, VP of Marketing at a 40-person DTC brand doing $15M/year"
- **Day in life:** Starts with checking ROAS in 3 different dashboards, reviews creative performance, meets with the agency, presents to the CEO on channel efficiency
- **Top pain points:** (1) Attribution is unreliable post-iOS (Score: 90), (2) Too many dashboards, no single source of truth (Score: 72), (3) Cannot justify ad spend increases to CFO (Score: 68)
- **Objections:** "We already have Triple Whale" → response with differentiation; "Our agency handles this" → response about agency independence
- **Buying triggers:** New fiscal year budget, disappointing Q4 results, agency change
- **Community intel:** Found in r/DTC, r/ecommerce, DTC Twitter, Operators podcast community
- **Voice quotes:** "I just want one dashboard that tells me the truth" (Reddit), "Triple Whale says one thing, GA4 says another, my agency says a third" (Facebook Group)
- **3 messaging angles** with headlines and proof points

## Example 2: DTC E-commerce Persona

**Prompt:**
> Create a buyer persona for our premium skincare brand. Our customer is a woman, 28-42, who spends $100+/month on skincare and shops primarily online.

**Expected output:** Consumer ICP profile:
- **Persona:** "Michelle, 34, Senior Product Manager, Brooklyn"
- **Day in life:** Morning skincare routine is a ritual, reads beauty blogs during lunch, follows skincare influencers on Instagram and TikTok, shops on brand websites (not Amazon for premium skincare)
- **Pain points:** (1) Ingredient confusion — too many options, contradictory advice (Score: 85), (2) Sensitivity — has wasted money on products that caused breakouts (Score: 78), (3) Trust — skeptical of influencer recommendations (Score: 65)
- **Community:** r/SkincareAddiction, r/30PlusSkinCare, Sephora community, TikTok #skintok
- **Voice quotes:** "I wish someone would just tell me exactly what to use for my skin type" (Reddit)
- **Buying triggers:** Seasonal change, running out of current product, friend recommendation, dermatologist visit
- **Messaging angles focused on trust, transparency, and simplicity**

## Example 3: Agency Services ICP

**Prompt:**
> Build an ICP for our performance marketing agency. We specialize in Google Ads and Meta for DTC brands doing $1M-$10M in revenue. They typically have 1-2 marketing people and no in-house paid media expertise.

**Expected output:** ICP profile:
- **Persona:** "Jake, Founder/CEO of a DTC brand doing $3M/year with 8 employees"
- **Top pain points:** (1) Tried running ads himself, wasted $15K (Score: 95), (2) Hired a freelancer who disappeared (Score: 82), (3) Does not know what good ROAS looks like for his category (Score: 70)
- **Objections:** "Agencies are too expensive" → cost of inaction calc showing $5K/month waste; "I want to keep it in-house eventually" → position as training wheels
- **Community:** DTC Twitter, Indie Hackers, Shopify community forums, Facebook groups for DTC founders
- **Buying triggers:** Black Friday/holiday prep (Aug-Sep), post-holiday slump (Jan-Feb), Series A funding
- **Messaging angles:** "Stop guessing with your ad spend," "We manage your ads so you can manage your business," "The agency that teaches you to fire them"
