# Competitor Ads Analyst — Examples

## Example 1: Competitor Ad Audit

**Prompt:**
> Analyze the Facebook ads of Klaviyo, Mailchimp, and Omnisend over the last 60 days. Focus on what pain points they're targeting and which creative formats they're using most.

**Expected output:** Competitive intelligence report with:
- 30-50 ads reviewed per competitor
- Pain point frequency table (e.g., "complexity" appears in 40% of Klaviyo ads, "price" in 60% of Mailchimp ads)
- Creative format breakdown per competitor
- Messaging clusters: "All three emphasize 'revenue attribution' — table stakes, not differentiator"
- Positioning map: Price vs. E-commerce Depth
- Gap finding: "None are targeting the 'agency' persona — opportunity for white-label messaging"

---

## Example 2: Messaging Gap Analysis

**Prompt:**
> I sell a project management tool for marketing teams. Analyze how Monday.com, Asana, and ClickUp position themselves in their Google Ads and find messaging gaps I can exploit.

**Expected output:**
- Messaging matrix: competitor x pain point x frequency
- All three emphasize "collaboration" and "one platform" — saturated angles
- Gaps identified:
  - None target "marketing-specific workflows" (campaign planning, content calendars)
  - None mention "agency/client collaboration" pain point
  - "Reporting to leadership" angle underused
- Creative brief: recommended messaging angles with headline suggestions for each gap
- Competitive positioning map showing white space for "marketing-specific" positioning

## Example 3: Creative Swipe File

**Prompt:**
> Build a swipe file from the top-performing ad formats I can find for DTC skincare brands on TikTok and Meta. I want to see what creative approaches are trending.

**Expected output:** Organized swipe file with 20-30 ads:
- Categorized by format: UGC (45%), Before/After (25%), Product Demo (15%), Stat Graphics (10%), Other (5%)
- UGC sub-patterns: "unboxing reaction," "get ready with me," "honest review"
- Copy patterns: leading with results ("In just 2 weeks..."), leading with problem ("I tried everything for...")
- Hook analysis: what the first 3 seconds look like in top video ads
- Trend notes: "Raw/authentic outperforming polished studio content 3:1"
- Actionable takeaways: 5 creative concepts to test, each inspired by patterns found
